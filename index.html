Photography Direction

Warm, hungry, confident — never cold, clinical or stocky.

05.1 Mood & Principles

Appetite, warmth,
and real texture.

01  Warm, directional key light — depth and sheen, never flat.

02  Real texture: juice, fruit pulp, condensation, skin.

03  Tight, sensory crops — get close enough to taste it.

04  Seamless orange or charcoal backdrops.

05.2 Product Photography

The pack is the hero.

DO

+  Single key light, soft fill, glossy seamless floor.

+  Stage the real fruit — mango, maracuyá — as proof of flavor.

+  Show the clear liquid; that's the whole point.

DON'T

–  Cluttered props or busy, multi-color backdrops.

–  Flat, shadowless catalog lighting.

dPTM Brand Book05 · Photography
05.3 Lifestyle Photography

Bodies, not bodybuilding.

DO

Sensory and human — skin, fruit, a single body in warm light. Confident, a little provocative.

DON'T

Default to veiny flexing, gym mirrors and grimacing reps. Use that energy rarely, and on purpose.

Section 12

Critical Assessment

An honest read on the current work — what stays, what sharpens, what goes.

12.1 The Verdict

Keep the fire. Fix the edges.

RETAIN

The name & double meaning · the orange · bold grotesque type · the keyline frame · the illustrated saga · the Clear product · the voice.

REFINE

Demote orange to accent · standardize the logo lockup & clear space · balance the aggressive body shots · commit to one grid.

ELIMINATE

Full-orange floods on every asset · oversaturation · clutter · profanity that's crude without a payoff.

REDESIGN

Re-build the packaging label artwork (garbled copy) · draw a proper icon set · publish a formal logo spec.

The instinct is genuinely strong. None of this is a rescue — it's a tune-up that turns a loud, lovable brand into a premium one that still sounds like itself.
